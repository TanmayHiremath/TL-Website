Projects

Braille Me

When Shyam Shah built his first simple prototype at Tinkerer’s Lab he was perhaps unaware that one day his Braille reading device will become the world’s most affordable, multi-language Digital Braille display. Even today 12 million Indians, the worlds largest blind population, are almost completely dependent on the braille script perforated on hard paper. Yet, in the modern world, most of the resources available are in digital form. Put simply, Braille Me is an input and output device wherein the content can be read and typed. You could insert a USB drive and this Braille device can read the files. You can pair it via Bluetooth to a Smartphone, computer, or tablet. You can also Whatsapp, send emails by typing messages in Braille Me which would then get converted into visual English (or another language) characters. The USP of BrailleMe is its unique magnetic operated actuators technology that enables them to offer the product at a 10x price advantage over competitors.

Metalman Automation

In 2014 when TL had just been set up Deepak Venkat and a team of students had begun exploring manufacturing processes, motion mechanization and computer integrated control by designing & building machine prototypes at TL from scratch and testing them iteratively to improve them further. By 2016 the team had successfully developed a working prototype of square tube profiling machine in Tinkerers lab. Today Metalman Automation is a Mumbai based industrial machinery startup incubated at SINE IIT Bombay. The company’s machinery and engineering support together gives shop-floors the ability to process metal in a way that allows quick completion of projects at a much lower cost.

Hanco

Hanco is an IoT device/headgear developed with intent to become a dedicated personal AI assistant. It also has text-to-speech support enabling the blind to read the same way as people with vision.

EmoTV

EmoTV steps towards making robots understand human emotion, thus lessening the gap of human machine interaction. The device is capable of involving itself in a conversation with a subject, and can determine moods. Running on python coded lines, it can efficiently involve in a conversation with a subject. It fea- tures playing songs alongside the emotion recognition part. As an added bonus, it shows up a cartoonized image of yours with your name written on bottom right of screen and displays your emotion. There is also an image magick gui alongside the cartoonized image for further editing of the image. The entire project is completely based on python scripts, displaying the vastness of the language. The project cov- ers topics ranging from machine learning using Tensorflow to Speech recognition and conversion.

6DOF Stewart Platform

The team has built a fairly precise platform with six degrees of freedom that has an error margin of about 5 degrees when it comes to rotation and 1 cm when it comes to translation.

Scribble

Scribble is a ‘smart pen’, which can convert handwritten scripts into editable text format in real time. The writer would get a soft copy of whatever he/she writes on the paper in the form of a text document without the requirement of scanning the paper or using some kind of a pressure sensitive surface.

Optikia

Optikia uses raspberry pi (a low cost credit card sized computer which you can also use for programming), to help the visually impaired by enabling them to read the text in front of the device, compose email, play audiobooks, tell date and time, send SOS etc.

Cardinique

Cardinique would be an effective Point-of-Care solution for patients with cardiac ailments. The extensive database encompasses a diverse range of cardiac diseases & symptoms, ensuring high accuracy with every medical case.
